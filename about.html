ABOUT

BasiCare Plus and BCx were created with the goal of making basic healthcare accessible and affordable. Led by experienced industry veterans, Brad Mullen and Eric LaRue, BCx is the first digital-first platform for obtaining and managing healthcare related services, such as prescription drugs and tele-med.

MORE

LaRue and Mullen built BasiCare Plus to fill a growing gap in basic healthcare for an expanding population. In 2013 while dining at a local restaurant where his daughter was working, Mullen was introduced to his daughter’s co-worker, Tara.

Tara was single mom who had recently lost her benefits and had had her hours cut so that the employer could meet the new requirements of the Affordable Care Act. Now working two jobs for full pay, neither job offered a healthcare benefit in which she could enroll. Mullen was working at that time in the health insurance industry and his daughter thought he could possibly steer the friend in the right direction.

Tara and people like her do not fear catastrophic health issues, they fear missing a car payment or rent because of minor health setbacks. Things like minor infections and viruses could require both a visit to the doctor for them or their child and very often, a medication, both of which do not fit into a working mom’s schedule or budget. Furthermore, these hard-working individuals have very few options for affordable and dignified care.

For the past several years, telemedicine programs have become more affordable and very popular. If a telemedicine service is designed correctly it can address 82% of all illnesses safely. However, a diagnosis does no good if you cannot afford the medication. LaRue and Mullen designed BasiCare Plus to provide telemedicine access with no co-pays and a feature that covers over 90% of the generic medications prescribed by the service. For Tara, this means that if she or her children become ill with one of the covered illnesses, there would be no charge for her doctor visit or prescription.
